# AntiLibrary

Topic: [The Anxiety of Too Many Things.md](./The%20Anxiety%20of%20Too%20Many%20Things.md)

An antilibrary is a collection of books that are owned but have not yet been read. The term was coined by Nassim Nicholas Taleb. The concept it describes has been compared to the Japanese tsundoku.

> a scholar conscious of the power of his antilibrary is not concerned with treating knowledge as a property to possess or consume; rather \[...\] how much you don’t know—and how to find out that information when you need it.

The term *antilibrary* was [coined](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neologism "Neologism") by [Nassim Nicholas Taleb](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nassim_Nicholas_Taleb "Nassim Nicholas Taleb") in his book *[The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Black_Swan:\_The_Impact_of_the_Highly_Improbable "The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable")*
