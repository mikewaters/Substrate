# RF Analysis Devices

1. **HackRF One**: An affordable software-defined radio (SDR) capable of transmitting and receiving signals from 1 MHz to 6 GHz. It's widely used for RF analysis and experimentation. 

2. **Keysight N9000B CXA Signal Analyzer**: A professional-grade spectrum analyzer offering a frequency range from 9 kHz to 26.5 GHz, suitable for detailed RF analysis. 

3. **Signal Hound BB60C**: A real-time spectrum analyzer covering 9 kHz to 6 GHz, known for its portability and real-time analysis capabilities. 

4. **RTL-SDR**: A budget-friendly SDR receiver that can monitor frequencies from 500 kHz to 1.75 GHz, ideal for hobbyists and basic RF monitoring.

5. **LimeSDR**: An advanced SDR platform supporting frequencies from 100 kHz to 3.8 GHz, suitable for a wide range of applications, including WiFi analysis.

6. **Kestrel Handheld RF Analyzer**: A portable device designed for detecting and analyzing RF signals, useful for fieldwork and quick diagnostics.

7. **BriteScan RF Detector**: A handheld RF detector that helps identify sources of RF interference, suitable for quick scans and identification.

8. **Cornet ED88T**: An EMF meter that measures RF radiation, useful for detecting electromagnetic interference in your environment.

9. **Trifield TF2**: A comprehensive EMF meter that measures electric, magnetic, and RF fields, aiding in identifying potential sources of interference.

10. **Wi-Spy DBx with Chanalyzer**: A WiFi spectrum analyzer that visualizes WiFi and non-WiFi interference in the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, helping to optimize your wireless network.

11. **Oscilloscope with RF Probes**: An advanced tool for capturing and analyzing RF signals in detail, suitable for in-depth troubleshooting.

When selecting a device, consider factors such as frequency range, portability, ease of use, and your specific requirements for detecting and analyzing RF interference.
