# Detect and analyze radio signals that might be interfering with Wifi

To detect and analyze aberrant radio signals that might be interfering with your WiFi, youâ€™ll need specialized equipment that can identify signals across a broader spectrum. Here are some suitable devices and tools:

### 1\. **RF Spectrum Analyzer**

- **What it does**: A spectrum analyzer scans the radio frequency (RF) spectrum to detect signals from all devices, including non-WiFi ones.

- **Recommended Models**:

   - **HackRF One** (affordable software-defined radio)

   - **Keysight N9000B CXA**

   - **Signal Hound BB60C**

- **Pros**: Offers detailed visualization of all RF activity, including noise and non-WiFi signals.

### 2\. **Software-Defined Radio (SDR) Receiver**

- **What it does**: An SDR receiver like the **RTL-SDR** allows you to tune into and analyze various RF signals using a connected computer.

- **Recommended Models**:

   - RTL-SDR (budget option)

   - LimeSDR (more advanced)

   - HackRF One

- **Pros**: Affordable and flexible; integrates with software like GNU Radio or SDR# for detailed analysis.

### 3\. **Handheld RF Detectors**

- **What it does**: These devices scan RF frequencies and alert you to high levels of interference.

- **Recommended Models**:

   - Kestrel Handheld RF Analyzer

   - BriteScan RF Detector

- **Pros**: Portable and easy to use for quick detection.

### 4\. **EMF Detectors**

- **What it does**: Measures electromagnetic fields, which can sometimes be indicative of RF interference.

- **Recommended Models**:

   - Cornet ED88T

   - Trifield TF2

- **Pros**: Useful for identifying general electromagnetic interference sources.

### 5\. **Wi-Spy DBx with Chanalyzer**

- **What it does**: Although primarily a WiFi spectrum analyzer, it can detect other 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz RF signals, such as those from cordless phones, microwaves, and Bluetooth devices.

- **Pros**: User-friendly and specifically optimized for troubleshooting WiFi interference.

### 6\. **Oscilloscope with RF Probes**

- **What it does**: Provides raw signal waveforms for detailed analysis.

- **Pros**: Advanced troubleshooting; useful if you need to analyze signal modulation or frequency.

### How to Use These Tools

1. **Scan Broad Frequencies**: Start with a wide-spectrum scan to locate any peaks or unusual activity.

2. **Focus on WiFi Bands**: Concentrate on the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands if WiFi interference is suspected.

3. **Identify the Source**: Use directional antennas or adjust settings to pinpoint the physical location of the interference.
