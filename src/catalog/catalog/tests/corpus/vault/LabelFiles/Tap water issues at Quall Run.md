# Tap water issues at Quall Run

Tap water turning green when I add iodized salt for boiling pasta Mar 19, 2025

Have the tap water checked for iron and copper.

When iodized salt is added to tap water, the green discoloration is typically caused by chemical reactions involving metals present in the water, particularly iron. Here's a breakdown of the key factors:

---

### **Iron Reaction with Iodized Salt**

1. **Electrolysis Effects**:\

   Tap water may contain ferrous (dissolved) or ferric (oxidized) iron\[3\]. When iodized salt (containing iodide) is added, chlorine/chloramine residuals in treated water can react with iodide, forming **iodinated disinfection byproducts (I-DBPs)**. ### **Copper Contamination**

In rare cases, green/blue water indicates **copper corrosion** from pipes\[6\]. However, this is unrelated to iodized salt unless the salt accelerates pipe corrosion. Copper levels exceeding 1â€“2 mg/L pose health risks and require plumbing inspection\[6\].

---

### **Key Takeaways**

- **Primary Cause**: Green water from iodized salt + tap water is most likely due to **iron reactions** (e.g., electrolysis or iron-rich water).

- **Safety**: While iron-related discoloration is generally harmless, I-DBPs formed during cooking with chlorinated water and iodized salt may pose health risks\[1\]\[4\].

- **Testing**: If green water persists without electrolysis, test for iron or copper levels and inspect plumbing\[3\]\[6\].

For practical solutions, avoid electrolysis with iron electrodes or switch to non-iodized salt if metals are present in your water supply.
