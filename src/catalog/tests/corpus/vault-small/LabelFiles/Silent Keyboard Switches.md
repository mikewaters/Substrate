### Silent Tactile vs Silent Linear

> Silent tactile and silent linear switches both have dampeners/padding materials added to reduce noise, but their fundamental mechanisms and resulting sound profiles differ:
>
> Silent Linear Switches:
>
> - Completely smooth keystroke with no bump
>
> - Usually slightly quieter overall since there's no tactile mechanism
>
> - Both downstroke and upstroke are dampened
>
> - Common examples: **Cherry MX Silent Red**, **Gateron Silent Red**
>
> - Generally considered the quietest option
>
> Silent Tactile Switches:
>
> - Has a small bump during keystroke (usually around 2mm down)
>
> - The tactile mechanism creates a tiny bit more noise than linear
>
> - Both downstroke and upstroke are dampened, but the tactile bump itself can produce a very subtle sound
>
> - Common examples: **Cherry MX Silent Brown, Gateron Silent Brown**
>
> - Still very quiet, but usually slightly louder than silent linears
>
> For pure sound reduction, silent linear switches are typically the better choice since they have fewer moving parts and no tactile mechanism to generate noise.
